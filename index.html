About Me

I recently defended my PhD dissertation in the Department of Horticultural Sciences at the University of Florida, under the guidance of Dr. Patricio Munoz in the Blueberry Breeding and Genomics Lab. My research employed a multidisciplinary approach to plant breeding, integrating phenotypic, phenomic, genomic, and environmental data. By leveraging advanced statistical and genetic methodologies, I developed tools for genomic and phenomic prediction, optimizing breeding strategies to enhance fruit quality traits and maximize genetic gains while minimizing costs.

Currently, as a postgraduate associate, I specialize in blueberry breeding and genomic data analysis, focusing on advanced phenotyping techniques and multi-trait selection. My work includes developing statistical models for genomic prediction in multi-environment trials, applying computer vision for yield estimation, acoustics for fruit texture analysis, and exploring the integration of phenomic-assisted selection in the breeding pipeline. Additionally, I collaborate with international partners to provide technical support for breeding programs and develop RShiny applications for interactive data visualization.
